Our Story

A Living Garden
in the Heart of Batumi


Batumi Central Park, affectionately known as "6 May Park," is the oldest and most beloved green space in all of Adjara. Nestled beside the shimmering Lake Nuri-Geli, it has been the city's living room for over 140 years.

Originally called Alexander's Garden when it was laid out by Prussian landscape artist Resler in 1881, the park grew to cover 78,000 square meters of lush subtropical greenery. Today it remains one of the most cherished destinations for both locals and visitors to Batumi.

The park is home to the Batumi Dolphinarium, a small zoo, lakeside paths, children's carousels, catamaran rides, and countless quiet corners perfect for picnics, conversations, and contemplation.

What to See

Park Attractions

From serene lakeside strolls to thrilling dolphin shows — the park has something for every visitor.

Batumi Dolphinarium

One of the park's most popular attractions, the dolphinarium offers spectacular shows with dolphins, sea lions, and other marine animals. A must-see for families and children.

Batumi Zoo

Located within the park grounds, the zoo houses a variety of local and exotic animals. While a popular attraction, visitors are encouraged to check reviews before visiting.

Lake Nuri-Geli

The park's centerpiece — a tranquil lake surrounded by a beautiful 1km walking loop. Catamaran and rowboat rentals are available, and ducks add to the peaceful atmosphere.

Children's Rides

Carousels, electric rides, and various family amusements scattered throughout the park, making it a favorite destination for children and families on weekends.

Botanical Paths

Wind through shaded pathways lined with subtropical species, ornamental shrubs, and tall trees — a living legacy of the park's 19th-century European landscape designers.

Cafés & Restaurants

Numerous cafés and eateries surround the park offering Georgian cuisine, fresh coffee, and local snacks. Perfect for a relaxing break after a morning stroll around the lake.

Through the Ages

A Rich History

1881

Foundation

Construction of Batumi's first public garden begins under the direction of Prussian landscape gardener Resler. The park is laid out on the southeastern shores of Lake Nuri-Geli, covering 78,000 square meters.

1884

The Work Pauses

Resler passes away in 1884, and construction is suspended for a year. The park remains unfinished but already shows the promise of a spectacular urban garden.

1885

French Touch

Renowned French landscape architect Mikhail Dalfons takes over and completes both the park and the adjacent boulevard. Rare subtropical shrubs and trees are planted, giving the garden its distinctive lush character.

1900s

Alexander's Garden

Named Alexander's Garden in honor of Russian Emperor Alexander III, the park becomes the crown jewel of Batumi's public life, beloved by residents and visitors alike during the city's cosmopolitan heyday.

1932

Cultural Park Era

The garden is reorganized as a Cultural and Recreational Park, then later transformed into a Children's Park and Pioneers' Camp during the Soviet period — serving generations of Batumi's youth.

2006

6 May Park

The park is officially renamed "6 May Park" and undergoes modernization. Today it remains Batumi's most cherished green space, attracting over 12,000 Google reviews with a 4.7-star rating.

For Visitors

Travel Tips

01
Arrive Early

The park is most magical in the early morning hours — misty lake reflections and birdsong before the crowds arrive. Perfect for photography and peaceful walks.

02
Free Entry

The park itself is completely free to enter and open 24/7. Some attractions like the Dolphinarium and zoo have separate ticket fees, so plan your budget accordingly.

03
Stray Dogs

You may encounter stray dogs in the park — don't be alarmed. They are mostly calm and harmless, and the city tags and vaccinates them. Simply give them space.

04
Bring Snacks

Pack a picnic! The lakeside is perfect for sitting on benches and enjoying local Georgian food. Many nearby bakeries sell fresh churchkhela, pastries, and fruit.
